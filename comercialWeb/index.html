WHAT IS IT?

The polybiome project is an initiative to reduce the cancerigenous potential of meat using microorganisms commonly found inside our body. It consists on a probiotic with 8 different organisms designed to transform harmful components of meat into harmless byproducts that will be naturally discarded by the body. But, how does it work?

Find out!

The problem:

In some daily foods there are molecules called polyamines, that we digest and absorbe at the intestinal tract. Polyamines are involved in cell proliferation and are normally found in small quantities in our body. However, it has been shown that a disbalance in the amount of polyamines is a factor of risk for cancer.

Next

WHICH FOODS CONTAIN MORE POLYAMINES?

Acording to the World Health Organitzation one every 33 cancers is ascribed to red or processed meat. In the Carcinogenic Classification Groups ranking processed meats are in the first risk group, and red meats in the second.

Next

How do we avoid them?

Once digested, the polyamines enter the blod floow and spread across the body, making them difficult to target. Therefore, it is nescesary an approach that eliminates this molecules before they are absorbed.

Next

Inside Job Approach

The microrganisms in Polybiome degrade the poliamines in the intestinal tract, reducing the external intake and reducing the risk factor of cancer.
